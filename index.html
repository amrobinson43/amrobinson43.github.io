Bio

I am a climatologist with expertise in urban heat, environmental modeling, and high-resolution geospatial analysis. My research has focused on quantifying how land-surface characteristics and atmospheric conditions interact to shape hyper-local heat stress patterns.

I develop modeling frameworks that integrate observational data, remote sensing, and machine learning to map environmental conditions at fine spatial scales. This work has supported improved understanding of urban heat exposure and the role of built and vegetated environments in shaping risk.

My broader focus is on building transferable data science and geospatial tools that connect environmental data to real-world applications, including planning, infrastructure, and decision support.
